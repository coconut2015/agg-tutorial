1 Anti-Grain Geometry Tutorial {#mainpage}
2 ========================================
3 
4 Anti-Grain Geometry (AGG) is a wellknown low level graphics library that can
5 produce very high quality 2D images. There are many projects use it as
6 the backend engine.
7 
8 This tutorial was created because AGG was somewhat difficult to use. One
9 basically has to look at other open source projects (e.g. matplotlib)
10 to figure out how to use some of its functionalities.
11 
12 This tutorial is based on AGG-2.4, which has a permissive BSD-license.
13 For the sake of ease of use, I also created an unofficial mirror at
14 https://github.com/coconut2015/agg-2.4 that strips away most non-essential
15 files.
16 
17 AGG Capabilities
18 ----------------
19 
20 AGG supports most operations in [SVG](https://www.w3.org/Graphics/SVG/).
21 In fact, it operates in a very similar fashion. Thus, it is very
22 straightfoward to create a higher level API that either generates
23 raster images or SVG files.
24 
25 For this reason, parts of tutorials will refer to SVG's capability
26 from time-to-time.
